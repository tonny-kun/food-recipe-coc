SINIGANG

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pork belly or pork ribs, cut into chunks
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 1-2 long green peppers (siling pang-sigang)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (patis)
  • 8 cups water or broth
  • 1 package sinigang mix (or 1/4 cup fresh tamarind paste)
  • 1-2 cups kangkong (water spinach) or other leafy greens
  • 1 medium eggplant, sliced into rounds
  • 1 medium radish (labanos), sliced
  • 1 small bunch string beans (sitaw), cut into 3-inch pieces
  • 1-2 potatoes (optional), sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

1. In a large pot, bring the water or broth to a boil. Add the pork and cook for about 10-15 minutes, skimming off the scum that rises to the top.

2. Add the onion and tomatoes. Lower the heat and let it simmer for about 30 minutes until the pork is tender.

3. Once the pork is tender, add the sinigang mix or tamarind paste. Stir to dissolve and let it simmer for another 10-15 minutes.

4. Add the eggplant, radish, and string beans. Simmer for about 5 minutes until the vegetables are tender.

5. Season the soup with fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste. Adjust the sourness with more tamarind paste or sinigang mix if desired.

6. Add the kangkong (water spinach) and green peppers. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.

7. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy!
