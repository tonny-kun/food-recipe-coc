DINUGUAN

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pork belly or pork shoulder, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 lb pork liver, blended into a smooth paste
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 Thai bird’s eye chilies (optional, for spice)
  • 1 1/2 cups pork blood (fresh, or available from a butcher or grocery store)
  • 1 cup vinegar
  • 1 1/2 cups water or broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

1. In a large pan or wok, sauté the garlic and onion until fragrant and softened.

2. Add the pork pieces and cook until browned on all sides.

3. Pour in the vinegar and bring it to a boil. Let it simmer for about 5-7 minutes to cook off the acidity.

4. Add the pork blood, soy sauce, and water (or broth) into the pan. Stir gently and let it simmer for 20-30 minutes until the pork becomes tender and the sauce thickens.

5. Season with salt, pepper, and bay leaves, adjusting the flavor to your taste. If you want extra heat, add the chopped bird's eye chilies.

6. Once the pork is tender and the sauce has thickened, stir in the blended pork liver. Let it cook for another 5 minutes, then taste for seasoning again.

7. Serve hot with steamed rice or puto (Filipino rice cakes). Enjoy!
